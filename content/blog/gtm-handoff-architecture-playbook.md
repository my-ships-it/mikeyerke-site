Most revenue friction lives in handoffs, not in individual team effort.

When leads, opportunities, and customer context move across teams without clear ownership and data contracts, execution slows and trust drops.

## Core architecture principles

1. Define entry and exit criteria for every major handoff
2. Normalize critical context before routing
3. Assign one accountable owner for each transition
4. Instrument SLA and exception visibility

## Failure modes to eliminate

- "Someone else owns this" ambiguity
- Context loss between systems
- Hidden queue time before action
- Inconsistent follow-up behaviors by segment

## Handoff quality metrics

- Time-to-owner
- Time-to-first-action
- Required-field completion
- Exception rate and resolution time

Handoff architecture is where strategy becomes execution speed. Get this right and everything downstream gets easier.
