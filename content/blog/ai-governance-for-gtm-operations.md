AI projects fail in GTM operations when speed outruns control design.

The fastest way to create operational debt is shipping AI-enabled workflow changes without ownership, fallback behavior, and quality review.

## A simple governance model

Use five checkpoints before broad rollout:

1. Workflow owner and accountable leader are named
2. Input boundaries and restricted use cases are documented
3. Human review rules are defined for low-confidence outputs
4. Escalation path is clear when errors occur
5. Monitoring cadence and owner are established

## Where teams usually get stuck

- Treating AI as a feature instead of a process change
- Skipping change management and enablement
- Measuring novelty instead of business outcomes

## Better launch criteria

- Is output quality measurable?
- Is rollback path documented?
- Does the workflow improve team throughput without hiding risk?

Governance is not bureaucracy. It is what lets AI scale safely in production environments.
